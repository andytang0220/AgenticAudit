## 1. Purpose & Scope

This policy governs reimbursable business travel and expenses for all Northwind Trading Co. ("Northwind") employees and contractors. Expenses that do not comply with this policy will not be reimbursed. Employees are expected to exercise good judgment and incur only reasonable, necessary, business-related costs.

## 2. General Rules

2.1. **Receipts** are required for **every expense of $25 or more**. Itemized receipts (not just credit-card summaries) are required for meals and lodging.

2.2. Expense reports must be submitted **within 30 days** of the trip end or the date the expense was incurred.

2.3. Expenses submitted **more than 60 days** after they were incurred will **not be reimbursed** except with written CFO approval.

2.4. Expenses must be charged to the correct cost center and accompanied by a clear business purpose.

## 3. Approval Thresholds

3.1. Individual expenses or reports up to **$1,000** require **direct manager** approval.

3.2. Expenses or reports over **$1,000 and up to $5,000** require **manager and department-head** approval.

3.3. Expenses or reports over **$5,000** require **director-level** approval in addition to the above.

## 4. Air Travel

4.1. Employees must book the **lowest reasonable economy fare** for flights with total scheduled flight time **under 6 hours**.

4.2. **Premium economy** is permitted for flights with total scheduled flight time of **6 hours or more**.

4.3. **Business class** requires prior **VP approval** and is permitted only for international flights of 8 hours or more.

4.4. **First class is never reimbursable.**

4.5. Flights should be booked at least **14 days in advance** where practical. Seat-selection and priority-boarding fees up to **$50 per flight** are reimbursable.

## 5. Lodging

5.1. Hotel room rates are reimbursable up to **$250 per night** in standard-cost cities.

5.2. In designated **high-cost cities** (New York, San Francisco, Boston, London, Tokyo, Singapore), the limit is **$350 per night**.

5.3. Room service is reimbursable within the daily meal limit. Mini-bar charges, in-room movies, and spa or fitness add-ons are **not reimbursable**.

## 6. Meals

6.1. Meals are reimbursable up to a per-diem limit of **$75 per day domestic** and **$100 per day international**, inclusive of tax and tip.

6.2. **Alcohol** is reimbursable only at client or team business meals, capped at **$30 per person**, and is never reimbursable on solo meals.

6.3. Meals already provided (e.g., included in a conference fee or a flight) are not separately reimbursable.

## 7. Ground Transportation

7.1. Taxis, ride-share, public transit, and airport shuttles are reimbursable for business travel.

7.2. **Rental cars** are limited to **economy or mid-size** classes. Premium, luxury, and SUV classes require prior manager approval.

7.3. **Personal vehicle mileage** is reimbursed at **$0.67 per mile**, supported by a mileage log; this rate covers fuel and wear, so separate fuel claims for a personal vehicle are not allowed.

7.4. Reasonable parking and toll charges are reimbursable with receipts where required.

## 8. Prohibited / Non-Reimbursable Expenses

The following are **never reimbursable**:

- Traffic, parking, or other fines and penalties
- Personal entertainment (movies, sightseeing, recreation)
- Mini-bar, in-room movies, and spa services
- Travel upgrades not authorized under this policy (first class, unauthorized business class, luxury rental cars)
- Personal items (toiletries beyond travel necessity, clothing, gifts for personal use)
- Late fees, interest, or finance charges on personal credit cards
- Expenses for spouses, family members, or other non-employees (unless a pre-approved business guest)
- Lost or unsubstantiated cash

## 9. Corporate Card

9.1. Corporate cards are for business expenses only. Personal use is prohibited.

9.2. Cardholders must reconcile statements monthly and attach all required receipts.
