## 1. Purpose & Scope

This policy governs how Northwind Trading Co. ("Northwind") purchases goods and services. It applies to all employees who request, approve, or commit Northwind funds. The goals are to ensure value for money, prevent fraud and conflicts of interest, and maintain a clear approval and audit trail.

## 2. Purchase Orders

2.1. A valid, approved **purchase order (PO)** is required for all purchases of **$1,000 or more** before any commitment is made to a vendor.

2.2. Employees may **not** commit Northwind to a purchase or sign a vendor order form without the approval authority defined in Section 3.

2.3. Invoices without a matching PO will be returned unpaid.

## 3. Approval Authority (by total purchase value)

| Purchase value | Required approval |
|----------------|-------------------|
| Up to $5,000 | Direct manager |
| $5,000 – $25,000 | Department head |
| $25,000 – $100,000 | Vice President **and** Finance |
| Over $100,000 | CFO (and competitive bidding per Section 4) |

3.1. Approval value is based on the **total** value of the purchase or contract over its full term, not a single installment.

3.2. **Splitting prohibited.** Purchases must not be divided into multiple smaller orders or requisitions in order to stay under an approval threshold or avoid competitive bidding. Multiple orders to the same vendor for related goods or services within a 90-day period are treated as a single purchase for approval purposes.

## 4. Competitive Bidding

4.1. Purchases over **$25,000** require at least **three (3) written quotes** from qualified vendors, retained with the requisition.

4.2. Purchases over **$100,000** require a formal competitive bid (RFP/RFQ) process managed by Procurement.

4.3. **Sole-source / non-competitive** purchases over **$10,000** require a written sole-source justification approved by the department head and Procurement (e.g., proprietary technology, sole authorized distributor, genuine emergency).

## 5. Approved Vendors

5.1. Purchases over **$10,000** must be placed with a vendor on the **Approved Vendor List**, or accompanied by a documented exception approved by Procurement.

5.2. New vendors must complete vendor onboarding and agree to the Vendor Code of Conduct (NW-POL-VND-001) before any award.

5.3. Vendors handling Northwind or customer data must additionally satisfy the Information Security & Data Protection Policy (NW-POL-SEC-002).

## 6. Contracts & Legal Review

6.1. Any contract, master services agreement, or commitment with a total value over **$50,000**, or with a term longer than **12 months**, requires **Legal review** before signature.

6.2. The following terms require Legal review regardless of value: uncapped liability, exclusivity, auto-renewal beyond 12 months, assignment of Northwind intellectual property, and indemnities running against Northwind.

6.3. Only authorized signatories listed in the Delegation of Authority may execute contracts on behalf of Northwind.

## 7. Conflicts of Interest

7.1. Any employee involved in a purchase must disclose any personal or family financial interest in the vendor.

7.2. Employees with a conflict must recuse themselves from the selection and approval of that vendor.

7.3. **Related-party** transactions (a vendor owned or controlled by a Northwind employee or their immediate family) require CFO approval and documented arm's-length pricing.

## 8. Payment Terms

8.1. Northwind's standard payment terms are **Net 45** from receipt of a valid, undisputed invoice. Terms more favorable to the vendor (e.g., prepayment, Net 15) require Finance approval.

8.2. Early-payment discounts should be captured where the economics are favorable.

## 9. Records & Audit

9.1. All requisitions, quotes, approvals, POs, and contracts must be retained for at least **7 years**.

9.2. Procurement may audit any purchase for compliance with this policy. Material non-compliance is reported to Finance leadership.
